Vector Addition Simulation - Examples

Try the following examples with the vector addition simulation, and try to answer the questions:

  1. Position vector A in any position, and then adjust the position of vector B so that the resultant is 0. Try several examples until you are ready to answer the question "What is the relationship between vector A and vector B when their resultant is 0?"
  2. Position vector A in any northwest position. Then adjust vector B until the resultant points directly west. Notice that there are many positions (theoretically, an infinite number of possible positions) that will accomplish this. What is the mathematical relationship between vectors A and B that will accomplish this?
  3. Repeat step 2, but this time try to adjust B until the resultant points directly north. How is this accomplished? What is the relationship between vector A and vector B now?
  4. Is it possible to adjust B so that the resultant will point directly south? How?
  5. Position vector A in any random position. Now adjust vector B until the magnitude of vector A + the magnitude of vector B = the magnitude of the resultant. Try other combinations. When does the sum of the magnitudes of the vectors equal the magnitude of the resultant?
  6. Repeat step 5 except try to find a relationship between vectors A and B that will produce a resultant whose magnitude is the difference between the magnitudes of the two vectors.
